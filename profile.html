Profile

At the beginning of the twentieth century, the spread of modern education and changing political circumstances on the global stage sparked an intellectual movement in India, which laid the foundation for numerous changes and movements at the social and political levels. The establishment of the Indian National Congress in 1885 catapulted the independence movement into a dynamic form. The beginning of the twentieth century saw a prolonged, massive, non-violent struggle for independence, led by Mahatma Gandhi , officially known as the "Father of the Nation" of modern India. In the same century, India's social movement , also a massive, non-violent, revolutionary struggle for social freedom, was led by Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar , who is known as the "Maker of Modern India," the "Father of the Constitution," and the "Messiah of the Dalits." Additionally, as a result of the revolutionary struggle led by Chandrashekhar Azad , Sardar Bhagat Singh , Sukhdev , Rajguru , Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose , etc. , India achieved complete independence from British rule on August 15 , 1947. Subsequently, on January 26 , 1950 , India became a republic.

India lies entirely on the Indian Plate, which is a subdivision of the Indo- Australian Plate . Anciently, this plate was part of Gondwanaland and was connected to Africa and Antarctica . Approximately 90 million years ago, during the Cretaceous period, the Indian Plate began moving northward at a rate of 15 cm/year and collided with the Eurasian Plate during the Eocene. The Tibetan Plateau and the Himalayan Mountains were formed by the uplift of sediments from the Tethys Trench between the Indian Plate and the Eurasian Plate . Subsequent accumulation of sediments in the frontal basin created the Indo-Gangetic Plain. The Indian Plate continues to move northward at a rate of approximately 5 cm/year, and the Himalayas are still rising in height at a rate of 2 mm/year.
